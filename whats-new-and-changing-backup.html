What’s New and Changing for 2018

Whether it’s our customers, our employees, or those who make up the communities where we work and live, people come first at PayPal. That’s why we’re introducing new benefits—and improving others—to help you take care of yourself and your family, recharge when you need to, and do your best work. Our new benefit programs help you with everything from managing your weight and improving your nutrition to reducing stress, starting a family, and more. Take a look at what you can expect in 2018.

Annual Enrollment is November 6–17

Make this the year you get to know your PayPal benefits. Our benefit programs are here for you every day. In 2018, you’ll have even more ways to nourish your physical, financial, and emotional health.


Expect More in 2018

We’ve got some good news to share for 2018:

  • Lower paycheck contributions for the UnitedHealthcare CDHP with Health Savings Account (HSA). Take a second look at the CDHP. You’ll pay less in employee paycheck contributions in 2018 than you paid in 2017. Looking for additional tax advantages? See how the HSA can help you boost your health care dollars.
  • No increase in paycheck contributions for the Kaiser HMO and dental and vision plans.
  • Contribute even more to your HSA. Build a bigger nest egg to cover current or future health care expenses. You can contribute up to $3,450 for individual coverage ($50 more than 2017) or up to $6,900 for family coverage ($150 more than 2017).
  • Save more in a Health Care Flexible Spending Account. You must enroll in FSAs each year during Annual Enrollment. You can contribute up to $2,600 ($50 more than 2017) to your Health Care FSA next year. Plus, you can carry over up to $500 in your Health Care FSA each year, so plan carefully when deciding how much to save. (Note: You can’t carry over any balance in the Dependent Care FSA.)

Medical
  • Changes to both UnitedHealthcare medical plans. Starting next year, you’ll see the following changes to the UHC medical plans:
    • Deductibles and out-of-pocket maximums will increase in the UnitedHealthcare CDHP with HSA to align with the market and help keep employee paycheck contributions affordable. We’ve passed along the savings from these increases by lowering CDHP paycheck contributions for 2018.
    • We’re removing the $25 reimbursement limit for out-of-network chiropractic and acupuncture services. The plan will pay 70% coinsurance after you meet the deductible for these services.
    • We’re removing the $350 per day maximum for out-of-network outpatient ambulatory surgery, TMJ, and orthognathic surgery.
  • Additional changes to the UHC $300 Deductible plan:
    • You’ll pay a flat $35 copay for specialty provider visits, including acupuncture, rehabilitation services, urgent care center visits and allergy testing.
    • You’ll now pay a $20 copay for your initial prenatal visit. Future visits are covered at 100%.
  • If you are managing diabetes, you’ll now pay nothing for doctors’ visits related to your care if you’re enrolled in a UHC medical plan. Plus, if you are enrolled in the dental or vision plan, you can also get an extra dental and vision exam for free. And don’t forget: Medications to treat your diabetes are covered at 100%!

Dental & Vision
  • We are changing the names of our dental and vision plans to Core and Enhanced based on your feedback. You’ll have the choice of the Core Dental plan or Enhanced Dental plan, and the Core Vision plan or Enhanced Vision plan.

    The enhanced dental plan provides coverage for additional benefits, such as a higher annual limit and orthodontia coverage. The enhanced vision plan offers you higher allowances for frames and lenses and a LASIK allowance. Find out more about what each plan offers, and choose the coverage that’s right for you.
    • Dental Option 1 > Core
    • Dental Option 2 > Enhanced
    • Vision Option 1 > Enhanced
    • Vision Option 2 > Core

Life & Disability Insurance
  • Plan for the unknown with Supplemental Life Insurance
    • During this Annual Enrollment period only, we’re increasing the Guaranteed Issue amount (the maximum you can elect without providing evidence of insurability, or completing a medical exam) as follows:
      • Optional employee life: Up to three times your salary or $500,000 (whichever is less)
      • Spouse life: $75,000
    • Revisit your life insurance needs to determine if your coverage is adequate. Don’t miss this rare opportunity to increase coverage without providing EOI!
  • Our new Life and Disability insurance carrier will be Prudential.

New! Benefits for the Whole You

The Whole You. It’s more than just the person you are at work. It’s your health, your finances, your family—your overall well-being. We’re here to support you, no matter what life throws your way. Take a look at some of the new programs available to you in 2018.

  • Lose weight for good! Real Appeal, a virtual weight-loss program, puts interactive videos, live online group discussions, and personalized coaching at your fingertips. You’ll have all the tools you need to make permanent lifestyle changes.
  • Get help with everyday life. meQuilibrium helps you build resilience to stress and reduce its negative effects through digital coaching.
  • Meet with a therapist—anytime, anywhere. Schedule a convenient virtual visit with a therapist in the privacy of your own home. Our Optum Employee Assistance Program (EAP) and UHC medical plans now offer virtual visits with professionals who can evaluate and help treat conditions like depression and anxiety. Optum’s Counseling Concierge Services can help you find a therapist near you who will meet your needs.
  • Family planning support—when you need it most. Pursuing fertility treatment can be complicated, emotionally draining, and expensive. Our new Progyny benefits can help you during this very personal journey through services such as egg freezing, IVF, and pre-implantation genetic screening if you are a UHC member. Progyny will also provide benefits for surrogacy and adoption support for all U.S. employees.
  • Have a healthier pregnancy. With the Grow Family Health Organizer app, soon-to-be mothers can track their progress, identify risks, and connect with providers—all from the convenience of a mobile phone.
  • Business travel for new moms just got easier. Milk Stork makes it possible for working moms to continue breastfeeding—even when traveling. Easily ship or carry refrigerated breast milk for your baby.

Before You Enroll

Follow these easy steps before you make changes to or enroll in benefits for 2018:

  • Watch the Annual Enrollment 2018 Preview video for a 90-second summary of our new programs!
  • Use the Medical Plan Cost Estimator to compare your medical plan options. See which plan is right for you in a few quick clicks.
  • View your 2018 benefit costs.
  • Read the Annual Enrollment FAQs.

Be sure to attend a Benefits Expo at your location to meet with plan representatives, and sign up for an upcoming webinar to learn more about Real Appeal, Progyny, meQuilibrium, UHC Telemental Health, and Milk Stork.

Ready to Enroll

When you’re ready to enroll, log in to YBR from November 6–17 to:

  • View your current coverage and 2018 options.
  • Add or update dependents.
  • Review/update your beneficiaries.
  • Make your benefit elections and confirm your enrollment by reviewing/printing your benefit summary.

Take Action!

If you don’t take action by November 17, you will have the same plans as before, except for your FSA. If you want to continue your FSA, you must re-enroll and choose your contribution amounts.
