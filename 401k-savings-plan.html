401(k) Savings Plan

Eligible employees may participate in the PayPal 401(k) Savings Plan at any time. You may contribute up to 50% of your eligible earnings up to the IRS limit. The maximum annual contribution set by the IRS is $18,500 for 2018. Under the 401(k) plan, you can designate some or all of your contribution as Roth contributions. PayPal will match 100% of your contribution, up to 4% of your compensation. Both employee and PayPal contributions are immediately 100% vested.

Your contributions are made each pay period from your eligible compensation. Eligible compensation includes base salary, hourly earnings, overtime pay, sales incentives, and bonus payouts to a maximum of $270,000.

Employees who will be age 50 and older at any time in 2018 may make an additional "catch-up" contribution of up to $6,000.

Review

Decide on a Plan Type. When you enroll in the PayPal plan, you have two options available to you:

  • Traditional 401(k). When you elect to contribute a percentage of your salary to your traditional 401(k) account, the money comes out before taxes are calculated. This way you reduce your taxable income now and only pay taxes when you withdraw the money.
  • Roth 401(k). With the Roth 401(k) option, contributions are deducted on a post-tax basis from your paycheck. Because you are taxed up front when Roth contributions are made, you will not pay taxes when the money and the earnings on that money are withdrawn, provided that you leave your money in the plan for at least five years following the year of your first Roth contribution and have reached age 59½, are disabled or are deceased.

Decide How Much to Save

You’ll need to decide what percentage from each paycheck you want to contribute to your 401(k). Keep in mind that PayPal offers a company match of up to 4% of your eligible compensation, with a maximum of $10,800. This means that for every dollar you contribute, PayPal will also contribute a dollar to your account, up to the yearly IRS maximum. This is “free money” for you—to help you save for your retirement! In addition, PayPal has a “true-up” feature. This feature ensures that you receive the maximum company match if you contribute at least 4% of your salary for the year, regardless of the timing of your deposits.

Enroll

Recent hires may enroll in the 401(k) plan on the Friday following their date of hire. Current employees may enroll at any time.

Start by visiting Your Benefits ResourcesTM (YBR) (@Work | Log In ). Then follow this enrollment checklist:

  • Set Up an Account. In setting up your account, you will need to know the following:
    • – Your user ID is your Social Security number.
    • – Your unique personal identification number (PIN) is initially defaulted to your four-digit month and date of birth. For example, if you were born on January 15, you would enter 0115.
  • Create a New PIN. Once you have logged on to your account, you will be asked to create a new password/PIN. You may also change your user ID.
  • Make Your Investment Election(s). You may invest your contributions in a variety of investment options with varying degrees of potential risk and return. The PayPal plan offers 16 investment choices.
  • Designate a Beneficiary. You'll need your beneficiary's name and address.
  • Complete Enrollment. Once you complete your enrollment, you will receive a confirmation letter in the mail or by email for the selections that you have made. Your contributions will be deducted automatically from your paycheck, and you will be able to see your contribution posted on the next applicable payroll cycle.
  • You are generally permitted to change the investment allocations of your existing account balances, change the investment selections for future contributions, or change your beneficiary(ies) as often as you like, though there may be some delays in processing.

You can also enroll by calling the Schwab Plan Hotline at 800-724-7526.

Invest

It can be tough to reach a goal when you don’t know which direction to go. To help you, you have access to a third-party investment advisory service, provided by Morningstar Investment Management LLC. With this advice, you can get:

  • Help determining your retirement savings goals
  • A savings and investment recommendation to help you work toward your goals
  • Consultation with an Advice Consultant who will walk you through key planning decisions related to your retirement goals

To schedule your consultation, reserve your preferred day and time online or call the Schwab Plan Hotline at 800-724-7526 between 4:00 a.m. and 8:00 p.m. Pacific Time, Monday through Friday.
