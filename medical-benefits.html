Medical Benefits

Staying healthy is everyone's priority. You do your part by taking care of yourself, and we provide a variety of valuable medical coverage options to help you protect the health of you and your family. You get to choose which plan best fits your needs and your budget.

Understanding your medical plan options

There are various medical plan options available to you:

  • $300 Deductible
  • Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP) with Health Savings Account (HSA)
  • HMOs (offered in some locations)
$300 Deductible
  • The $300 Deductible option is a plan that uses a network of providers who have agreed to accept the UnitedHealthcare (UHC) payment schedule for covered services.
  • You have flexibility to see any provider, anywhere—you decide whether to use in- or out-of-network providers, but you pay less when you use in-network providers.
  • If you use an out-of-network provider, you're responsible for any billed charges that exceed "customary and reasonable" charges.
  • There is no charge for covered preventive care services—they’re covered at 100% by the plan.
  • Each covered person must meet his or her individual deductible, up to the combined family deductible. Then the plan’s coinsurance is applied.
  • Medical copays, deductibles and coinsurance apply to the out-of-pocket maximum (OOPM). Once you meet your OOPM, the plan pays 100% for covered services through the end of the year.
  • Pharmacy benefits are provided by CVS/caremark.
  • For more information or to find a provider, check out welcometouhc.com/paypal.
Consumer Directed Health Plan with Health Savings Account (CDHP with HSA)
  • The CDHP with HSA is a high-deductible health plan. That means that except for preventive exams, all care (including prescriptions) is subject to the deductible and coinsurance.
  • You have the option to make tax-free contributions to an HSA to pay for qualified medical expenses or save for future medical expenses. The company also makes an annual contribution to the HSA ($450 for individuals or $900 for family). Learn more about the HSA.
  • The deductible applies to all services except preventive care.
  • The entire family must accumulate to the family deductible before any individual reaches coinsurance.
  • This plan costs less out of your paycheck than the $300 Deductible plan.
  • Pharmacy benefits are provided by CVS/caremark.
  • Review the FAQs for the CDHP with HSA option.
  • To learn more about the CDHP with HSA, review the CDHP video.
  • Visit welcometouhc.com/paypal for additional details on the CDHP with HSA, such as confirming network providers and using the Medical Plan Cost Estimator.
HMO plans
(available only in AZ, CA and UT)

In a health maintenance organization (HMO), you and each covered dependent must choose a primary care physician (PCP) within the HMO network, who coordinates all of your care with providers within the HMO network.

  • You pay copays only, no coinsurance.
  • If you go outside the network, you do not have any coverage except in emergency situations.
  • The following plans are available in these states:
    • Arizona: Health Net HMO
    • California: Kaiser HMO
    • Utah: SelectHealth HMO

Which medical plan is right for you?

The PayPal medical plans offer comprehensive coverage and cover you at 100% for in-network preventive care. As you're deciding on the plan that's right for you, keep in mind these key considerations that can help you choose:

  • Consider your options using the Medical Plan Cost Estimator. In just a few short clicks, you can see which plan is better for you financially, from what you’ll pay out of your paycheck to what you’ll pay when accessing care.
  • How much health care did you use last year? How much do you think you'll need this year? Do you have a surgery planned? Having a baby? Think about your medical plan needs for the coming year.
  • Did you meet your deductible last year? Or did you pay more for coverage you didn't use?
  • If there's a deductible, you'll have to meet it before the plan pays benefits. How much does the plan pay versus how much you'll need to pay (your coinsurance), and how much could you have to pay in one year (out-of-pocket maximum)?
  • Must you use network providers to receive benefits? Does the plan cover out-of-network providers?
  • How much does the plan cost each pay period? See your Employee Contributions.

Take Advantage of Convenient Virtual Care

Get access to care online—at any time of day or night, from anywhere! Don’t have time to go to your doctor’s office? Up all night with a sick child? A virtual visit lets you see and talk to a doctor from your mobile device or computer without an appointment. Used for non-emergency medical conditions, virtual visits can save you time and money when you or your family needs care for common medical concerns.

Virtual Visits are available to all UnitedHealthcare members. Stay mobile with the UnitedHealthcare Health4Me app and access your health plan on the go. UnitedHealthcare members also have convenient access to therapists through online video conferencing. You can speak with a clinician who can evaluate and treat conditions such as depression and anxiety. They can also provide therapy and, when appropriate, prescribe medications.

Kaiser Permanente members (HMO available to CA employees only) have the option of video visits for a secure way to access care for minor health conditions. Check out kp.org/mydoctor/videovisits for more information.

ConnectCare, SelectHealth’s virtual care provider, is available 24/7. Visit with a doctor via secure, high-quality streaming video from home or on the go. Visit intermountainhealthcare.org and search ConnectCare.

To view other options for quick and virtual care, view the Benefit Summary Documents page.

About the Health Savings Account (HSA)

If you enroll in the UnitedHealthcare CDHP, you can open a Health Savings Account (HSA) through HealthEquity. Once you enroll in the CDHP, you’ll receive a welcome kit from HealthEquity with information about your Health Savings Account.

You can use the HSA to pay for qualified medical expenses, including deductibles and copays—or keep it for future expenses, even into retirement.

  • Your contributions to the HSA are tax-free. You can select a contribution amount upon enrolling, and pre-tax contributions will be made through Payroll.
  • PayPal also contributes to your HSA: $450 annually for employee-only coverage and $900 annually if you cover dependents. (The annual contribution will be included in the first paycheck issued following enrollment in the plan.)
  • The maximum total annual contribution (yours and PayPal's) to your HSA is $3,450 for employee-only coverage and $6,900 if you cover dependents. You can contribute an additional $1000 if you are age 55 or older.
  • You earn tax-free interest or investment returns on your HSA balance.
  • Any money in your HSA that you don't spend stays in your account to help you save for future medical and retiree health care expenses.
  • Your HSA belongs to you—you take it with you if you leave the company or retire.
  • You will receive a debit card following activation of your HSA.

Watch a short video about the CDHP with HSA option and review some frequently asked questions.

Thinking about having both an HSA and a Health Care Flexible Spending Account (FSA)?

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA and plan to enroll in the Health Care FSA, the IRS says reimbursement under the FSA will be limited to reimbursement of dental and vision expenses only.
