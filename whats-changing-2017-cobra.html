What's Changing for 2017

Before you make any benefit decisions, find out what's changing for 2017. Click a topic below to learn more.

Medical plan changes

There are a few changes to the medical plan options for 2017: a new medical insurance carrier, a new name for the PPO 300 (now called the $300 Deductible plan), and the previously announced elimination of the PPO 750 plan.

New medical insurance carrier: UnitedHealthcare

Beginning January 1, 2017, UnitedHealthcare (UHC) will replace Blue Shield of California as the national insurance carrier for the Preferred Provider Organization plan with $300 deductible (now called the $300 Deductible plan) and the Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP) with HSA medical plan options. This change does not affect:

  • Prescription drug benefits under the $300 Deductible and CDHP with HSA medical plan options, which will continue to be provided by CVS/caremark. You'll have an additional way to get your maintenance drugs.
  • The HMO plans, which will remain available to employees in Arizona (Health Net), California (Kaiser) and Utah (SelectHealth).

Get an overview of the move to UHC.

What the move to UHC means for you

The $300 Deductible and CDHP with HSA medical plans will work the same way under UHC as they currently do under Blue Shield of Californiaâ€”same deductibles, copayments, coinsurance, out-of-pocket maximums, etc. And because there is significant overlap between the UHC and Blue Shield of California provider networks, most participants will be able to continue seeing their current in-network doctors. (As it was with Blue Shield, you still have your choice of providers, but you will pay significantly less by using in-network providers.)

If you enroll in the $300 Deductible or CDHP with HSA medical plan for 2017:

  • Check to see if your physicians are part of the UHC network. Visit http://welcometouhc.com/paypal to do a provider search.
  • Expect to receive a new medical plan ID card with a new member ID and a new group number (Group # 909006) from UHC in December. After December 15, additional cards can be requested on myuhc.com or from Customer Service by calling 344-298-2737.

Transition of Care

If you or a covered family member is in a current course of treatment for a serious medical condition (for example, a surgery scheduled for January 2017, in the last trimester of pregnancy, ongoing treatment for a significant health condition), please contact UHC at 844-298-2737 to discuss Transition of Care. The UHC team will assist you and/or your family member and guide you on the best way to ensure continuity of treatment and care, including how to request Transition of Care. UHC may approve continued treatment by the current doctor for a limited time if UHC determines it is medically necessary that you continue treatment with your current physician.

Please visit the "Transition of Care" section of http://welcometouhc.com/paypal.

PPO 300 plan

The PPO 300 medical plan option will now be called the $300 Deductible plan to better reflect the differences between it and the Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP) with HSA medical plan.

PPO 750 plan

The PPO 750 plan will no longer be offered beginning January 1, 2017. If you are currently enrolled in the PPO 750, you can choose to enroll in the $300 Deductible or the CDHP with HSA medical plan (or a regional HMO, if available to you).

Prescription enhancements: More flexibility for getting maintenance medications

Do you take maintenance medications on an ongoing basis (for example, to help control high blood pressure, diabetes, asthma)? If so, you can already get a 90-day supply at a reduced cost by using the mail-order option. Beginning January 1, 2017, you can also choose to pick up a 90-day supply of maintenance drugs at your local CVS/caremark or Target retail pharmacy. The price is the same as mail order: a 90-day supply for the cost of a 60-day supply (it's like getting a month's supply for free).

Watch a video about your options for getting maintenance medications.

If you don't take action during Annual Enrollment and you are currently enrolled in a Blue Shield of California medical plan, you will automatically be enrolled in the $300 Deductible plan, covering the same dependents you currently cover, assuming you continue to make timely payments for your COBRA coverage and remain eligible.

Changes to the Health Savings Account (HSA)

New HSA banking partner

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA medical plan, you'll have a new banking partner for your Health Savings Account beginning in January 2017. The HSA will now be offered through Optum Bank.

If you currently have an HSA with Health Equity, you'll receive information in mid-December about activating your account with Optum Bank, transferring your account balance, and receiving your new Optum Bank HSA debit card.

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA for the first time, you'll receive information in mid-December about opening your Optum Bank HSA, receiving your HSA debit card, and using your account.

2017 limit for HSA contributions

The IRS has increased the individual maximum HSA contribution from $3,350 to $3,400 for 2017.

Total 2017 contribution limit
Individual coverage $3,400
Family coverage $6,750

If you will be age 55 or older in 2017, you can make an additional "catch-up" contribution of $1,000.

New EAP provider

Beginning in January, Optum EAP will become the new provider of our Employee Assistance Program (EAP). Through the Optum EAP, you and your family members can talk to an expert who can help you:

  • Manage stress, anxiety, depression and addiction.
  • Handle relationship issues and divorce.
  • Develop your resilience.
  • Cope with illness and death.
  • Manage your finances and create a financial plan.
  • Access legal advice from a licensed attorney.

This benefit provides you and your eligible family members with six free, confidential counseling sessions (by phone or in person) per issue per year at no cost. Once you've completed your six free sessions, additional sessions may be covered under your medical plan at the specialist rate.

Note: Arbor Family Counseling will remain the EAP provider in Nebraska.
