2018 Time Off Benefits

PayPal offers a variety of time off benefits to help you enjoy some special moments in your life or just to recharge when you need it.

Paid leaves of absence

We believe that taking care of yourself and your family is the most important thing you can do. That’s why PayPal provides the following paid leave programs:

  • Enhanced maternity leave (for birth mothers), in addition to disability leave
  • Bonding leave (for mothers, fathers, same-sex spouses and domestic partners)
  • Family care leave
  • Sick leave

Disability leaves of absence

If you can’t work because of an illness or injury, you can take time off to heal. Your income is protected under PayPal’s disability insurance plans.

  • Short-Term Disability (STD) — If you are away from work for longer than seven calendar days as a result of an illness, injury, medical emergency, surgery or childbirth (including pregnancy disability and postpartum recovery), you may be eligible for salary continuation of up to 80% of your base salary, up to a maximum of $6,500 per week for up to 6 months. There is a 7-day waiting period for benefits.
  • Long-Term Disability (LTD) — If your condition restricts your ability to work for more than six months, the LTD program provides benefits of 67% of your base salary, up to $25,000 per month.

Note: Your disability benefits may vary depending on laws, your circumstances, and other factors.

Time off programs

PayPal provides Paid Time Off (PTO) for non-exempt employees and Tracking-Free Vacation (TFV) for exempt employees.

  • Non-exempt employees can use Paid Time Off (PTO) for vacation, personal time, or illness. You begin to accrue PTO from your first day of employment. If you’re a full-time employee, your PTO accrues at 4.92 hours per pay period, with an additional day added for each year of service, up to 20 days per year. If you’re a part-time employee, your PTO accrual will be pro-rated based on your scheduled hours.
  • Exempt employees follow our Tracking-Free Vacation (TFV) policy when they want to take time off. TFV means you manage your time off, and work with your supervisor to schedule time away. TFV does not accrue or have an annual limit.

Sabbatical

A sabbatical provides an extended break (four weeks off!) from the pace and intensity of your work, and allows you to spend time with your family, travel, pursue a hobby or interest or just simply relax.

Full- and part-time employees are eligible to request a four-week paid sabbatical after completing five years of continuous service. The sabbatical must be taken on a continuous basis (all four weeks at once).
