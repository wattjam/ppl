What's Changing for 2017

Before you make any benefit decisions, find out what's changing for 2017. Click a topic below to learn more.

Medical

New medical insurance carrier: UnitedHealthcare

Beginning January 1, 2017, UnitedHealthcare (UHC) will replace Blue Shield of California as the national insurance carrier for the Preferred Provider Organization plan with $300 deductible (now called the $300 Deductible plan) and the Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP) with HSA medical plan options.

New medical carrier but the same $300 Deductible Plan and Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP)

The $300 Deductible and CDHP with HSA medical plans will work the same way under UHC as they currently do under Blue Shield of California—same deductibles, copayments, coinsurance, out-of-pocket maximums, etc. (You may notice that the PPO 300 medical plan option will now be called the $300 Deductible plan to better reflect the differences between it and the Consumer Directed Health Plan (CDHP) with HSA medical plan.)

UnitedHealthcare providers

Because there is significant overlap between the UHC and Blue Shield of California provider networks, most participants will be able to continue seeing their current in-network doctors. (As it was with Blue Shield, you still have your choice of providers, but you will pay significantly less by using in-network providers.) Check to see if your physicians are part of the UHC network. Visit http://welcometouhc.com/paypal to do a provider search.

Transition of Care

If you or a covered family member is in a current course of treatment with a provider who is not in the UnitedHealthcare network, you may be eligible for transition of care services, which enable you to continue with your non-network provider for a limited time. Examples of situations that would be considered for transition of care are: a surgery scheduled for January 2017 at a non-UHC network facility or with a non-UHC network provider; the last trimester of pregnancy with a non-UHC network physician; an ongoing treatment for a significant health condition with non-UHC network providers.

Please contact UHC at 844-298-2737 to discuss Transition of Care. The UHC team will assist you and/or your family member and guide you on the best way to ensure continuity of treatment and care, including how to request Transition of Care. UHC may approve continued treatment by the current doctor for a limited time if UHC determines it is medically necessary that you continue treatment with your current physician. Please visit the "Transition of Care" section of http://welcometouhc.com/paypal.

PPO 750 plan

The PPO 750 plan will no longer be offered beginning January 1, 2017. If you’re currently enrolled in the PPO 750 plan, you’ll automatically transition to the $300 Deductible plan unless you make another selection. If you’re comfortable moving to the $300 Deductible plan, you don’t need to do anything. You may also choose a regional HMO, if available to you.

Prescription enhancements: More flexibility for getting maintenance medications

Do you take maintenance medications on an ongoing basis (for example, to help control high blood pressure, diabetes, asthma)? If so, you can already get a 90-day supply at a reduced cost by using the mail-order option. Beginning January 1, 2017, you can also choose to pick up a 90-day supply of maintenance drugs at your local CVS/caremark or Target retail pharmacy. The price is the same as mail order: a 90-day supply for the cost of a 60-day supply (it's like getting a month's supply for free).

Watch a video about your options for getting maintenance medications.

If you don't take any action during Annual Enrollment, here’s what will happen:

  • If you’re currently enrolled in the PPO 750, you’ll automatically be enrolled in the $300 Deductible plan with UnitedHealthcare, covering the same dependents you currently cover.
  • If you’re currently enrolled in the PPO 300 or CDHP with HSA, you’ll automatically be enrolled in the same plan ($300 Deductible or CDHP with HSA) with UnitedHealthcare, covering the same dependents you currently cover.
  • If you’re currently enrolled in one of the HMO plans, your election will not change.

New ID cards for UnitedHealthcare

Expect to receive a new medical plan ID card with a new member ID and a new group number (Group # 909006) from UHC in December. After December 15, additional cards can be requested on myuhc.com or from UHC Customer Service by calling 844-298-2737.

HMO plans will remain available to eligible individuals in Arizona (Health Net), California (Kaiser) and Utah (SelectHealth).

Consumer Directed Health Plan with Health Savings Account

New HSA banking partner

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA medical plan, you'll have a new banking partner for your Health Savings Account beginning in January 2017. The HSA will now be offered through Optum Bank.

If you currently have an HSA with Health Equity, you'll receive information in mid-December about activating your account with Optum Bank, transferring your account balance, and receiving your new Optum Bank HSA debit card.

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA for the first time, you'll receive information in mid-December about opening your Optum Bank HSA, receiving your HSA debit card, and using your account.

2017 limit for HSA contributions

The IRS has increased the individual maximum HSA contribution from $3,350 to $3,400 for 2017.

Total 2017 contribution limit
Individual coverage $3,400
Family coverage $6,750

If you will be age 55 or older in 2017, you can make an additional "catch-up" contribution of $1,000.

New vision plan options

The 2017 vision plan now offers you a choice of coverage options through VSP. Option 1 offers higher allowances and provides coverage for LASIK surgery, while Option 2 has a lower premium each month.

Please note: For Option 1, the new $300 allowance is shared between frames and lenses, so you could spend $300 on frames, or $300 on lenses, or $150 on each. You have a $150 allowance to spend on a first pair of frames or lenses, and then another $150 to spend on a second pair of frames or lenses. The maximum allowance is $300.

New EAP provider

Beginning in January, Optum EAP will become the new provider of our Employee Assistance Program (EAP). Through the Optum EAP, you and your family members can talk to an expert who can help you:

  • Manage stress, anxiety, depression and addiction.
  • Handle relationship issues and divorce.
  • Develop your resilience.
  • Cope with illness and death.
  • Manage your finances and create a financial plan.
  • Access legal advice from a licensed attorney.

This benefit provides you and your eligible family members with six free, confidential counseling sessions (by phone or in person) per issue per year at no cost. Once you've completed your six free sessions, additional sessions may be covered under your medical plan at the specialist rate.

Note: Arbor Family Counseling will remain the EAP provider in Nebraska.
