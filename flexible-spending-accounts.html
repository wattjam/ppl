Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs)

Flexible Spending Accounts can help you save money on eligible health care and dependent care expenses. You essentially "bank" your pre-tax contributions in a tax-free account that you can use to pay for expenses not covered by other benefit programs—which can make your health care and dependent care dollars go further.

You must enroll in FSAs each year during Annual Enrollment. Your enrollment and contribution amount do not carry over from year to year.

How the FSAs work
  • There are two separate FSAs—one for health care and one for dependent care. You can enroll in one or both. Your Spending Account™ (YSA) administers both accounts.
  • You set aside payroll deductions to pay for eligible expenses during the calendar year. For employees hired during the year, your benefits effective date is your date of hire.
  • You can contribute up to $5,000 per year in the Dependent Care FSA and up to $2,600 per year in the Health Care FSA. You don't pay federal or state income taxes on FSA contributions.
  • As you have eligible health care expenses throughout the year, you can use the YSA debit card (see below) or reimburse yourself from your Health Care FSA—tax-free! This reduces your taxable income and puts more money back in your pocket. Review a list of eligible expenses.
  • The PayPal Health Care FSA has a carryover feature. Up to $500 of your year-end Health Care FSA balance each year will automatically be added to your new plan year balance. You can use these carryover funds for reimbursement of eligible health care expenses incurred anytime throughout the year. There is no carryover feature for the Dependent Care FSA.
  • Plan carefully. There's a deadline for spending money in your FSAs. All claims must be submitted by April 30, 2018, to be reimbursed using 2017 contributions.
  • FSAs are subject to IRS rules. Be sure to save all your receipts. You may be asked to provide copies to confirm your expenses. If you do not provide receipts when requested, your YSA debit card could be deactivated.

Note: Under federal tax law, PayPal's FSA programs must pass certain nondiscrimination tests each year. These tests are designed to ensure that highly compensated or key employees/participants do not receive disproportionately greater benefits. If necessary, the maximum participant contribution may be reduced midyear to comply with federal law. This qualifies as a life event, and you may want to consider splitting the maximum amount under your spouse's FSA program, if available.

PayPal helps with dependent care expenses (for non-exempt employees only)

If you’re a non-exempt employee and you contribute to a Dependent Care Flexible Spending Account, PayPal will contribute an additional 15% to your account, up to $652 annually, to help you pay for eligible expenses. You may contribute as little as $50 or as much as $5,000. Your annual contribution limit includes your contribution and PayPal’s 15%.

About the YSA debit card

All Health Care FSA participants with an annual election of $100 or more will receive a debit card from Your Spending Account™ (YSA). You can use the card to pay for eligible health care expenses anywhere the debit card is accepted. That way, you don't have to pay for eligible health care expenses out of your own pocket. When you use your YSA card, your eligible expenses are automatically deducted from your account.

How the CDHP affects your Health Care FSA

If you enroll in the CDHP with HSA plan and plan to enroll in the Health Care FSA, reimbursement under the FSA will be limited to reimbursement of dental and vision expenses only, because you are already receiving a tax benefit on medical expenses through your HSA. Learn more about how the CDHP with HSA and your Health Care FSA differ and how they work together.
