Prescription Drug Benefits

Prescription drugs are important to good health—and a significant driver of health care costs. Use your prescription benefits wisely, and always ask for a generic equivalent to save money. Just as with the medical plans, there are restrictions on which pharmacies to use, and there are financial advantages to using a network pharmacy.

Your prescription drug benefits depend on the medical plan you choose. HMO plans each manage their own prescription drug benefits, while CVS/caremark provides prescription drug benefits for participants in the UnitedHealthcare (UHC) plans.

PayPal wants to ensure you and your family members have no obstacles to receiving the care you need. That's why employees covered under UHC, Kaiser Permanente (CA) and Health Net (AZ) medical plans will receive in-network maintenance drugs at no cost to help manage these chronic medical conditions:

  • Diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • High cholesterol

All medical plans offered by PayPal provide coverage at no cost for certain preventive anti-cancer medications for women over age 35 with increased risk of breast cancer, and annual screenings for certain individuals at risk for lung cancer, including CT scans of the thorax.

Compare Your Prescription Drug Benefits

Retail prescriptions are for up to a 30-day supply of medication. If you're on a maintenance medication for an extended period (for example, for high blood pressure), you should consider the mail-order option, which provides a larger quantity for a reduced copay. UHC participants can pick up maintenance prescriptions at their local CVS or Target pharmacy in addition to using the mail-order drug program.
